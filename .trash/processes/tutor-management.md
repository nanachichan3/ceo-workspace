## Purpose

This document is the operations manual for managing Self-Degree tutors — the people who deliver the company's tutoring service. The book (Chapter 7) covers *why* one-on-one tutoring works. This document covers *how* to run the tutoring operation: hire, train, support, and fill tutors with students consistently.

---

## The Model

**Core philosophy:** tutors are not teachers. They are learning catalysts. Every design decision flows from this.

Bloom's 2 Sigma research (1984) showed that 1-on-1 tutoring produces learning outcomes at the 98th percentile vs. classroom at the 50th. The three ingredients are:
1. **Immediacy** — catch errors in real-time
2. **Personalization** — teach the learner, not the curriculum
3. **Accountability** — no place to hide

Our tutors are trained to deliver all three. Our operations are designed to make that sustainable.

## PILLAR 1: HIRING

### 1.1 Who We're Looking For

**Not:** certified teachers, academics, people who want to lecture.
**Yes:** people who are themselves self-directed learners, who understand the Socratic method, who can read a learner's confusion and respond to exactly it.

**Required traits:**
- Demonstrated self-directed learning in their own background (built something, learned something hard without a classroom)
- Socratic temperament — they ask questions, not just answer them
- Patience with frustration — they can sit with a learner's confusion without solving it for them
- Clear communication — they can explain a concept two ways, know which way maps to which learner
- Growth mindset — they reflect on sessions and iterate

**Nice to have:**
- Subject matter expertise in a domain the company serves
- Prior tutoring or mentoring experience
- Familiarity with AI tutoring tools (Khanmigo, Claude, etc.)

### 1.2 Sourcing Channels

Ranked by expected ROI:

| Channel | Effort | Volume | Quality |
|---------|--------|--------|---------|
| Inbound from book readers (email list) | Low | Low | High |
| LinkedIn outreach to bootcamp grads | Medium | Medium | High |
| Reddit r/self-taught, r/learnprogramming | Low | Medium | Medium |
| University career centers (part-time tutors) | Medium | High | Medium |
| Upwork / Fiverr (screening required) | Low | High | Low |
| Referrals from existing tutors | Low | Low | Very High |

**Primary channel:** Book reader inbound. The Self-Degree book attracts exactly the right person. Every reader email list signup is a warm tutor lead.

**Secondary channel:** Targeted LinkedIn DMs to bootcamp grads and autodidacts who are 1-3 years into their self-taught journey — they remember what learning hard things felt like.

### 1.3 Interview Process

**Stage 1: Application form** (10 min)
Ask three questions:
1. Describe a time you learned something difficult without a teacher or formal instruction. What made it work?
2. A learner you tutoring is stuck on a concept you find obvious. What do you do?
3. You've been tutoring someone for 3 sessions and they're not improving. What do you do?

Scoring: looking for self-awareness, Socratic instinct, and evidence of genuine learning struggle (not performance).

**Stage 2: Mock tutoring session** (30 min)
Give the candidate a learner profile (a fictional student with specific gaps) and 30 minutes to prepare + run a session. Observe:
- Do they lead with questions or answers?
- Do they adjust when the learner doesn't follow?
- Do they let the learner sit with confusion, or do they rush to explain?
- Do they check for understanding before moving on?

Score rubric (1-3 per dimension):

| Dimension | 1 (Fail) | 2 (Pass) | 3 (Excellent) |
|-----------|----------|---------|---------------|
| Socratic vs. lecturing | Explains without asking | Asks some questions | Leads entirely with questions |
| Reading confusion | Misses confusion signals | Notices obvious confusion | Notices subtle confusion |
| Pacing | Too fast or too slow | Adjusts eventually | Adjusts in real-time |
| Accountability | Lets gaps slide | Notes gaps | Addresses gaps directly |

**Pass threshold:** Average 2.0 across all dimensions with no single dimension below 1.5.

**Stage 3: Values check** (15 min)
Short conversation about the Self-Degree philosophy. Look for:
- Genuine buy-in to self-directed learning (not just lip service)
- Alignment with the "guide on the side" role
- Comfort with ambiguity — learners won't always have clear goals

### 1.4 Offer & Contracting

- Contract type: freelance / independent contractor (not W-2)
- Contract template: standard independent contractor agreement with IP clause and no-solicitation clause (tutors may not take learners off-platform)
- Trial period: first 5 sessions evaluated, then permanent status
- Required: completion of Tutor Certification before first paid session

## PILLAR 2: EDUCATION (Tutor Certification)

### 2.1 Purpose

Certify tutors in the Self-Degree Method — the specific approach this company uses for tutoring. This is not generic tutoring training. It's training in the method grounded in Chapter 7's pedagogy and adapted for operational scale.

### 2.2 Certification Structure

**Module 1: The Science of Tutoring** (async, ~2 hours)
*Foundational knowledge — must be completed before any live session.*

- Read: Chapter 7 of the Self-Degree Framework book
- Watch: Bloom's 2 Sigma Problem explainer (embedded video, ~20 min)
- Quiz: 10 questions on key findings (must score 80%+ to pass)
- Deliverable: written reflection on "What makes tutoring different from teaching?"

**Module 2: The Self-Degree Tutor Method** (async + live, ~4 hours)
*Practical application of the method.*

- Core principles:
  - **The Guide on the Side** — tutors don't deliver content; they structure experiences
  - **The 3-Feedback Framework** — immediacy, personalization, accountability
  - **Socratic, not instructive** — questions over explanations, every time
  - **The Hard Part Test** — "Is the learner doing the hard part, or am I?" (adapted from book)
  - **Metacognitive check-ins** — tutors regularly ask learners to assess their own understanding

- Video examples: 3 annotated session clips (good/better/best) with commentary
- Role-play: conduct a 20-min mock session with a partner playing a frustrated learner
- Debrief: submit recording for coach review (or peer review if no coach available)

**Module 3: Platform & Operations** (async, ~1 hour)
*Tools, booking, notes, communication protocols.*

- How to use the scheduling system
- Session note template and when to write one
- How to flag a learner who's not progressing (escalation path)
- Communication norms (response time, async feedback windows)
- Data privacy and learner information handling

**Module 4: Live Certification Session** (live, 45 min)
*Final demonstration.*

- Run a 30-min mock session with a real learner (matched by the company)
- Tutor receives written feedback within 48 hours
- Pass/fail on the same rubric as hiring Stage 2, with 2.0 average required
- If fail: one retake allowed after 1 week of practice

### 2.3 Certification Timeline

```
Day 0:        Offer extended → receives onboarding link
Days 1-7:     Module 1 (async, self-paced)
Days 8-14:    Module 2 (async + peer role-play)
Days 15-18:   Module 3 (async)
Day 19:       Live certification session
Day 21:       Certification result + activated as "Tutor-in-Training"
Day 21-35:    First 5 trial sessions (supervised, paid at Level 1 rate)
Day 35+:      Permanent active status OR contract ended if trial fails
```

**Tutor-in-Training phase (Days 21-35):**
During the first 5 trial sessions, the tutor is active and paid but:
- Each session note is reviewed by a Senior Tutor or Coach within 24 hours
- Learner is told this is a trial-period tutor (transparency)
- Tutors in Training do not count toward the active tutor roster for student assignment purposes
- Utilization minimum (3 sessions/week) does not apply during the trial period

Total: ~5 weeks from offer to fully active status.

## PILLAR 3: SUPPORT

### 3.1 Keeping Tutors Effective

Tutor dropout and burnout are the two biggest operational risks. The support system has three layers:

**Layer 1: Async Feedback (always on)**
After every session, tutors submit a session note (5 min max) via the platform:
- What worked / what didn't
- Learner's state at start and end
- One thing to try next session
- Flag if concerned (triggers proactive outreach)

Operations team reviews notes weekly. Any flag triggers a check-in message within 48 hours.

**Layer 2: Weekly Tutor Office Hours** (optional, 30 min/week)
Live drop-in call (Zoom/Meet) hosted by a senior tutor or the company's education lead. Tutors share challenges, get coaching in real-time. This also builds culture and reduces isolation.

**Layer 3: Monthly Tutor Performance Review**
Monthly check-in per active tutor:
- Session count and utilization (are they getting enough students?)
- Learner progress data (where available)
- Self-reported satisfaction and concerns
- 1-on-1 with ops lead (30 min)

### 3.2 Compensation Structure

**Base hourly session rates (by level):**

| Level | Title | Base Rate (USD/hr) |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Associate Tutor | $25–$35 |
| 2 | Certified Tutor | $30–$45 |
| 3 | Senior Tutor | $40–$60 |
| 4 | Tutor Coach | $50–$75 |

Rates are set at hiring based on subject expertise, experience, and interview performance. Use the lower end for new hires, move up the scale after 3 months of consistent 5-star learner feedback.

**Milestone bonus:** $10/session bonus when a learner hits a milestone. Milestones are:
- Defined collaboratively at the first session (tutor + learner agree on 1-3 specific, observable outcomes)
- Recorded in the learner's session notes
- Marked complete when the learner can demonstrably perform the milestone task independently

**Async pay:** $5 per session for written session notes submitted within 24 hours of a session. This is a flat fee paid on top of the session rate — it is additive, not a replacement. Applies to every session where a note is submitted; capped at one async payment per session even if multiple notes are written.

**Minimum commitment:** 3 sessions/week to maintain active status

**Pay frequency:** Bi-weekly, paid on the Friday following the end of each bi-weekly billing period.

### 3.3 Escalation Paths

```
LEARNER ESCALATION FLOW

Learner is disengaged (2+ sessions without progress)
    → Tutor flags via session note
    → Ops team reaches out to learner within 48h
    → If no improvement after 1 more session → suggest learner try different approach

Learner needs level beyond tutor's expertise
    → Tutor flags in session note
    → Ops team arranges tutor swap or refers to external resource

Tutor is struggling with a learner
    → Tutor requests support via async note
    → Ops team matches with peer tutor for co-session or supervision
    → If persistent: 1-on-1 coaching session within 1 week

LEARNER COMPLAINT ESCALATION

Learner reports inappropriate conduct by tutor
    → Ops team pauses all sessions immediately
    → Response to learner within 24 hours
    → Internal review within 48 hours
    → Outcome: warning, contract termination, or full investigation depending on severity

Learner disputes a charge
    → Review session notes (all sessions documented)
    → Resolve within 5 business days
    → If material dispute: issue credit or refund at ops lead discretion

Learner requests mid-arc cancellation
    → Remaining sessions refunded in full, no questions
    → Exit survey (async, 3 questions — what went wrong, what worked, would you recommend)
    → Tutor notified of learner cancellation via session note flag
```

### 3.4 Tutor Career Ladder

Average active learners per tutor: **6–8 learners** (assuming ~2 sessions/learner/week across the roster)

| Level | Title | Active Learners | Weekly Sessions | Scope | Pay Multiplier |
|-------|-------|----------------|-----------------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Associate Tutor | 3–5 | 6–10 | Direct learners only | 1x |
| 2 | Certified Tutor | 5–8 | 10–16 | Direct learners + peer mentoring | 1.25x |
| 3 | Senior Tutor | 8–10 | 16–20 | + module delivery in certification | 1.5x |
| 4 | Tutor Coach | — | — | Runs certification, mentors L1–2 | 1.75x + coaching stipend |

Tutors advance by submitting peer feedback and session performance data. No arbitrary promotion timelines.

**Inactive status (voluntary pause):**
Tutors who need to go inactive temporarily (travel, personal) may place themselves on hold for up to 30 days per year without losing active status. Learners are reassigned during the pause. After 30 days, the tutor's slot is posted for a replacement and the returning tutor goes to the bottom of the matching queue.

## PILLAR 4: STUDENT FLOW (Consistently Finding Students)

### 4.1 Why This Is Separate from Marketing

Most tutoring companies conflate marketing with student flow. The distinction:
- **Marketing:** brand awareness, content, ads — generates interest
- **Student flow:** the systematic process of turning interest into a booked session

Student flow is an operations problem, not a marketing problem. We solve it with systems.

### 4.2 The Student Flow Pipeline

```
Interest (inbound or outbound)
    ↓
Qualification (is this person a fit for Self-Degree tutoring?)
    ↓
Discovery Call (15 min, free) — understand the learner's goals, gaps, history
    ↓
Match (assign learner to best-fit tutor based on subject, personality, schedule)
    ↓
First Session (paid, 60 min) — assess and set learning plan
    ↓
Learning Arc (ongoing) — regular sessions with progress milestones
    ↓
Renewal / Referral — happy learners refer peers
```

### 4.3 Interest Sources (Ranked by LTV of student)

| Source | Volume | Close Rate | Student Quality | Notes |
|--------|--------|------------|-----------------|-------|
| Book reader email list | Low | High | Very High | Warm. Already believes in the philosophy. |
| SEO / Blog (self-degree.com) | Medium | Medium | High | Intent-rich content. |
| Podcast / YouTube (brand content) | Medium | Low-Medium | High | Top of funnel. Needs longer nurture. |
| Referral from existing learner | Low | Very High | Very High | Highest LTV. Cheapest to acquire. |
| Reddit / Community (r/learnprogramming etc.) | High | Low | Medium | Volume but low close. |
| Paid ads (Google, LinkedIn) | High | Low-Medium | Medium | Expensive. Use only when other channels are maximized. |

**Primary focus:** Book reader inbound + referrals. These two channels should receive 60%+ of ops attention.

**Expected volume:** For the first 6 months, plan for 2–4 qualified learner inquiries/month from book inbound + 1–2 referrals/month. This is sufficient to hire and sustain 1–2 active tutors. Scale hiring pipeline before launching paid ads.

### 4.4 Qualification Criteria

Not every interested learner is a fit. Qualifying questions for the discovery call:

1. What are you trying to learn / accomplish? (Goal clarity)
2. How have you tried to learn this before? (Approach history — red flag if they say "YouTube only")
3. How many hours per week can you commit to learning? (Availability — min 3h/week recommended)
4. What's your budget range? (Price fit)
5. Have you tried self-directed learning before? (Self-awareness — do they know it's hard?)

**Disqualify if:**
- Looking for someone to just give them answers (not a fit for Socratic method)
- Expecting miracles in weeks (wrong expectations)
- Can't commit minimum 3h/week to actual learning work
- Price incompatible after discovery (need to be direct, not shame them)

### 4.5 Learner Pricing

Session pricing is set by the company, not by tutors. The ops manager quotes from this structure during the discovery call:

**Per-session pricing (learner pays):**

| Tutor Level | Price per 60-min session |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Associate Tutor | $60–$80 |
| Certified Tutor | $75–$100 |
| Senior Tutor | $95–$130 |
| Tutor Coach | $120–$150 |

The exact price within the band is set at intake based on learner budget and session frequency commitment.

**Starter package (new learners only):**
- 4-session starter pack, paid upfront: 5% discount vs. per-session rate
- Purpose: reduces churn in the first month by committing the learner to a short arc

**Monthly package (ongoing learners):**
- 8-session monthly pack, paid upfront: 10% discount vs. per-session rate
- Purpose: sustainable revenue cadence for ops; learners commit to a month of consistent work

**Discovery call:** The 15-min discovery call is free. At the end, if both parties are aligned, the ops manager quotes the applicable rate and offers the starter or monthly package. No pressure sales — if the learner wants to think about it, they go into a 14-day nurture sequence (email cadence, not spam).

### 4.5 Tutor-Student Matching

Match on four axes:
1. **Subject expertise** — primary axis. Don't assign a web dev beginner to a math tutor.
2. **Schedule compatibility** — both parties must have overlapping availability
3. **Learner personality fit** — some learners need patience, others need directness
4. **Tutor workload** — avoid overloading tutors above 10 active learners simultaneously

Use a concrete matching scorecard — score each factor 0–10, apply weight, sum:

| Factor | Weight | Scoring Guide |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Subject match | 40% | 0 = wrong subject, 5 = adjacent subject, 10 = exact match |
| Schedule overlap | 30% | 0 = no overlap, 5 = partial overlap, 10 = full schedule match |
| Personality fit | 20% | 0 = known mismatch, 5 = unknown/default, 10 = documented strong fit from session notes |
| Tutor workload | 10% | 0 = at 10-learner cap, 10 = <4 active learners (under-utilized) |

**Threshold:** Highest total score wins. If no tutor scores 6.0+ weighted average, flag for ops manual review.

**Who scores:** Ops team does the scoring at match time. Tutor workload score is derived from the active learner count in the scheduling system.

### 4.6 Keeping Students (Reducing Churn)

Churn usually happens for three reasons:
1. **No visible progress** — learner doesn't feel they're improving
2. **Too expensive for vague value** — without milestones, the ROI feels unclear
3. **Bad personality fit** — learner doesn't trust or like the tutor

**Countermeasures:**

*Progress visibility:*
- Session notes go to learner too (they see what's been flagged and addressed)
- Every 4th session: a structured check-in where tutor and learner assess progress against the goals set at the start
- Milestone document: after every 4 sessions, write down 2-3 concrete things the learner can now do that they couldn't before

*Value anchoring:*
- At the end of every session, tutors state: "Today we worked on [X]. If a learner is stuck on [X], they should now be able to [specific task]."
- This creates a running record of value delivered. Learners see their own growth.

*Relationship:*
- Tutors are trained to ask about the learner's goals beyond the subject — what are they trying to build or become?
- This surfaces intrinsic motivation and reduces the "is this worth the money" spiral.

### 4.7 Student-to-Tutor Ratio

**Active learner slots per tutor:** 6–8 learners per tutor across the full roster.

**Sessions:** Each learner averages 2 sessions/week (range 1–3, set at intake based on learner availability and goals). This gives a tutor with 6–8 learners a natural weekly session load of **12–16 sessions**.

**Why 6–8 and not 10+:** At 10 active learners × 2 sessions/week = 20 sessions/week. That's a heavy load for a freelance tutor and risks burnout. 6–8 is the sustainable range.

**Capacity table:**

| Active Learners | Sessions/Week (avg 2/learner) | Utilisation benchmark |
|----------------|-------------------------------|----------------------|
| 3 | 6 | 50% — low, may need reassignment |
| 6 | 12 | 75% — target minimum |
| 8 | 16 | 100% — at limit |
| 10+ | 20+ | Overloaded — flag for review |

Ops monitors utilization weekly. If a tutor drops below 12 sessions/week (75% utilisation) for 2 consecutive weeks: check in and consider reassigning 1–2 learners.

**Tutor workload cap:** No tutor may carry more than 10 active learners simultaneously, regardless of level.
